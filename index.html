WHO AM I?

Born in the south of Sweden back in 1993, when the eyebrows were too thin, jeans too low waisted and tops too cropped - But also the decade that gave brith to the society we know and cherish today.

I got my first computer at the age of 6 and I remember my first computer game was Snoopy's Game Club. Let's just say it had some basic graphics and 8-bit music in all its' gloriousness. It helped me with a lot of things such as learning how to count Woodstocks and to find Charlie (the owner of Snoopy) who was hiding behind pumpkins... Oh wait! I know just what you're thinking! It's very valueable knowledge indeed.

But the most valueable lesson of it all, is that it taught me at a very young age that computers were (and still are, for that matter) the future.


... AND HOW DOES THIS MAKE SENSE??

In my early teens is when everything really kicked off. I started capturing photographs and manipulating them in Adobe's infamous Photoshop. I was getting more and more curious about other software such as programs that could record me playing on my electronic piano.

I used to hang out on MySpace and among all the emos (and all the other attention seeking people), I noticed that some of the pages were really different and simply cooler than the others. So since I was anything but cool I wanted to learn how to do just that (Whatever it was that they were doing to create that pink unicorn cloud when you hover over the "contact section").

I couldn't think of anyone else to ask other than my super hacker dad since he was the one to give me a fake Photoshop license key. He basically gave me a 5 minute tutorial on HTML and told me "Now, play around with it!". So I did and I slowly discovered so much more!

Now, more than 10 years later I feel like it is about time I make this more than a hobby. So please use this page as a reference. It has really brought back all the feels. FeelsGoodMan.
